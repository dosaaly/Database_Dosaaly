*core concepts*
Term	
Definition
Table	
A collection of data organized in rows and columns.
Row	
A single record representing one specific item/entity.
Column	
A category of information (field) that applies to every row.
Primary Key	
A unique identifier for every row; no two rows can share the same value.
Foreign Key	
A column that refers to a primary key in another table to create a relationship.
Schema	
The overall blueprint or structure of the database.

*sql and nosql*
SQL Databases: Feature rigid schemas, vertical scaling, and strict adherence to ACID principles (Atomicity, Consistency, Isolation, Durability). They are preferred for complex transactions and financial systems
NoSQL Databases: Use flexible schemas (e.g., Document, Key-value, Columnar, or Graph) and horizontal scaling. They are ideal for large volumes of unstructured data and rapid scaling

*postgresql* 
PostgreSQL is highlighted as a robust, open-source DBMS used by major corporations
